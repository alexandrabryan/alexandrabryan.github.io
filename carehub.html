The Carehub Web App

A web app for communication and coordinating care and events among a private social network for caregivers.

Built by Team 2 of MomsCan:Code Develop Camp. See Carehub in action

Project Overview

This project was created by Team 2 from MomsCan:Code Develop Camp, cohort 1. It is a collaborative web app built by a remote team of 4 moms-turned-coders as a result of a 3 week sprint project, using the Human-Centered Design Thinking process.

We were presented with a fact ("The elderly will outnumber their children for the first time in US history.") and followed the design thinking process to eventually identify a problem with our target user, and prototype a solution to that problem. The design thinking steps involved doing initial research, creating a stakeholder map, empathizing with the user, data collection via suverys and interviews, defining a problem and ideating solutions. In about a week, we had gone from the fact above to a "big idea" to wireframe and prototype.

Our pitch - "Our team of web developer moms is building a web app to help caregivers of aging family members easily build a support network with a single hub to communicate and coordinate care."

We then used Figma to create a low fidelity wireframe to outline the user experience and main functionality of the app, then got to work! The app was deployed via Heroku, and uses PostgreSQL as its database.

My Role

My role in this project was primarily as the team lead. Research and Process

After an initial research process, we conducted an online survey of over 200 people from around the world. Our goal was to design and develop a product to help people regain connection in their lives and feel less lonely and isolated. We were particularly interested in the the segment of participant aged 45+.

It is important to note here, that at the time of the survey, we did not know what we were going to build. While we knew we wanted to center a solution around connection, we did not know who our target audience would be for our product, but rather looked to use our survey results to inform about the problems faced by this segment and how they used both on and off line avenues to make connections in their lives currently.

We conducted a survey to identify key issues facing the aging population. Based on our survey, of 244 total responses (all age groups) 114 of them feel isolated at least some of the time. And of those 114 people who feel isolated, 101 of them interact in person with friends and family either a few times a week or every day. Narrowing the problem down further, we identified that the communication in caring for an aging family member can be confusing, sporadic, and disproportionately fall on one or two people. We want to give caregivers the means to form a small, private network of communication all in one place. This will empower the elder to remain connected and involved while giving the caregiving network a way to support and communicate effectively with both the elder and the network.
